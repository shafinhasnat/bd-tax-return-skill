# Investment Tax Rebate (Section 78, Schedule 6 Part 3)

**Source:** Finance Ordinance 2025 (KPMG); PwC Bangladesh worked example
**Applies to:** Both AY 2025-26 and AY 2026-27 (no change)

---

## Formula

**Tax rebate = LOWEST of these three:**

| | Component |
|---|---|
| (a) | 3% of total taxable income (excluding tax-exempt income and income taxed at reduced/final rates) |
| (b) | 15% of actual eligible investment (per Schedule 6, Part 3) |
| (c) | **BDT 10,00,000** (1 million / 10 lakh) absolute cap |

```
rebate = MIN(0.03 × taxable_income,  0.15 × eligible_investment,  10_00_000)
```

## Eligible Investments (per IT-11GA Schedule 5)

1. **Life Insurance Premium** or Contractual Deferred Annuity paid in Bangladesh (own, spouse, or dependent children)
2. **Contribution to Deposit Pension Scheme (DPS)** — *practical cap: BDT 1,20,000/year per scheduled bank or financial institution* (per NBR rules, not statute)
3. **Investment in Government Securities, Unit Certificate, Mutual Fund, ETF or Joint Investment Scheme Unit Certificate**
4. **Investment in Securities listed with Approved Stock Exchange** (DSE/CSE)
5. **Contribution to Provident Fund** to which Provident Fund Act, 1925 applies (general PF)
6. **Self & Employer's Contribution to Recognized Provident Fund** (private-sector RPF)
7. **Contribution to Super Annuation Fund**
8. **Contribution to Benevolent Fund / Group Insurance Premium**
9. **Contribution to Zakat Fund** (approved fund)
10. **Others** as prescribed

## Worked Example (per PwC AY 2026-27)

- Taxable income: BDT 11,47,470
- Eligible investment: BDT 4,65,000
- (a) 3% × 11,47,470 = **34,424**  ← lowest
- (b) 15% × 4,65,000 = 69,750
- (c) 10,00,000
- **Rebate = BDT 34,424**

## Caveats / Things to Verify with the Taxpayer

- **DPS BDT 1,20,000 cap** is per individual scheme — confirm with NBR Paripatra for current year.
- For **Sanchaypatra (savings certificates)** — purchase qualifies as investment for the year, but interest income is generally subject to final-discharge withholding tax and may not enter taxable income for rebate-base purposes.
- Investment must be made **during the income year** (1 July – 30 June), not the assessment year.
- Receipts/proofs are mandatory — store originals; eReturn allows uploads.
